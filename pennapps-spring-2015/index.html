At PennApps Health.
Visualization and healthcare?

What if we apply visualization to schedulers in the hospital (pigeonholing the number of patients who need treatment into the limited number of rooms in a hospital)

Iron Triangle

Healthcare is suffering from a fingers-pointing problem that prevents anybody from taking responsibility for supporting innovative technologies. Prototypes for low-hanging fruit suffer in user adoption because no one wants to take responsibility for paying for it. It's an formidable problem that's keeping innovation from making it past these interlocking gates of healthcare. Elliot Menschik suggests two solutions:
1. Focus on a target audience that removes the complexity from the equation (e.g. conceierge doctors that are able to develop a customer experience for their patients)
2. Take one side of this triangle that suffers from excessive overhead, and convince the other two sides of the triangle how painfully obvious it is to adopt this newer point on the triangle (e.g. Oscar)
